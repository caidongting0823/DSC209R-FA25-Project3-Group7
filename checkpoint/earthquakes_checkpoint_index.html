A Century of U.S. Earthquakes: Interactive Map of 100 Years of Earthquake Activity (Mw≥4.5)

Group 7 Dongting Cai, Albab Newaz, Chiu-Chiu (Jojo) Lin, Conner Houghtby
Course DSC 209R, Project 3

Key Question

“What are the patterns in the frequency and magnitude of earthquakes across different regions of the United States over the past 100 years? (Mw≥4.5)”

Project Goal (Final Submission)

Build an interactive D3.js map of U.S. earthquakes over the past 100 years (including Alaska, Hawaii & Puerto Rico/USVI). Each quake is a dot: larger for higher magnitude and darker for greater intensity. Hover tooltips reveal details. Users can filter by magnitude and by region (states or broader zones).

Checkpoint Note: Plots below are quick generated using Python Package (acceptable for the checkpoint). The final interactive will be D3-only.

Dataset

  • Source: USGS ANSS ComCat Earthquake Catalog: Using USGS API
  • Time range: 100 years (January 1, 1925 - November 2, 2025)
  • Region: United States, including Alaska, Hawaii, Puerto Rico, and the US Virgin Islands
  • Checkpoint subset: M ≥ 4.5
  • Fields used: time, latitude, longitude, magnitude, depth, place, region
  • Data Insights: 11,859 earthquakes recorded in the filtered dataset (M≥4.5) over the past century.

Planned Interactions (Final D3.js)

  • Circle size ∝ magnitude; circle color encodes intensity.
  • Hover tooltips: magnitude, place, date, depth.
  • Filters: magnitude range slider; region picker (states & zones).
  • Optional zoom/pan and a size/color legend.
